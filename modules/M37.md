# M37 — Missions 7–10

Track: Authoring · Depends: M36 · **Founder work, ~5 days**
**Do not start this until M36's gate passed. If people didn't pay at mission 6, more missions won't fix it.**

## Goal
Depth. The learner can now change a live system without fear.

## The four

| # | Slug | Capability | Checks |
|---|---|---|---|
| 07 | `build-a-real-feature` | Form → DB → list, in production | `http_ok` POST then GET |
| 08 | `protect-a-route` | Not everything is public | `http_ok`: 401 anon, 200 authed |
| 09 | `ship-a-change` | Push → live in 2 minutes | commit sha in prod matches HEAD |
| 10 | `custom-domain` **(optional)** | DNS and HTTPS aren't magic | `remote_https_valid` on their domain |

## Mission 9 is quietly the most important

Everything before it was a one-time achievement. Mission 9 is the **habit**. A learner who can change production and watch it deploy in two minutes has stopped being someone who took a course and become someone who ships.

The check: their deployed `/api/health` returns a commit sha matching their local `git rev-parse HEAD`. Elegant, unfakeable, and it teaches them that production has a version.

## Mission 10 must be optional and must say so

Frontmatter: `optional: true`. It gates nothing (M15's unlock rules already handle this).

The prose opens by saying it costs about ₹1,000/year of their own money and their app is already live without it. **Then** teach DNS properly — nameservers, A vs CNAME, why propagation is slow, why HTTPS needs the DNS first.

And the check will return `PENDING` for hours (M21). That's not a bug — it's the mission's actual lesson: **some of production is just waiting, and waiting isn't failure.** Write that sentence in the mission.

## Journey completion

`journey.yaml` completion copy:
> **You shipped it.**
> Your app is live. You built it, you deployed it, and you can do it again tomorrow without anyone's help.

No certificate. No badge. No PDF. Product Bible: *"The final outcome is confidence. Not certificates."*

## Founder gate
1. Build passes. ☐
2. **Do all ten yourself, fresh folder, only your words.** ☐
3. The five people from M36 finish 7–10. ☐
4. **At least one of them says something like "I could do this again on my own."** That's the Definition of Success from your own PRD §92 — capability, not engagement. If nobody says it, the journey taught steps instead of understanding, and you need to go back to the reflections. ☐

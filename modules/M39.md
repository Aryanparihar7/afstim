# M39 — Legal pages

Track: Launch · Depends: M06 · Blocks: — · Agent time: ~2h

## Goal
You can legally take money from a stranger in India.

## Files you may touch
- `src/app/(marketing)/terms/page.tsx`
- `src/app/(marketing)/privacy/page.tsx`
- `src/app/(marketing)/refunds/page.tsx`

## Spec

**Privacy — India's DPDP Act 2023 applies to you.** Not a formality; it's law and you're a data fiduciary. State plainly:

- **What we store:** email, name (optional), password hash, your mission progress, your reflections, URLs you submit, questions you ask the mentor.
- **What we don't:** your code. Your files. Your repo contents. Afstim never reads your project — the CLI runs on your machine and sends back pass/fail, nothing else.
- **Who else sees it:** Anthropic (mentor questions + error text you paste), Resend (your email address), Neon (the database), Vercel (server logs).
- **How to delete it:** email you. You do it by hand. At 20 users that's honest, not lazy.
- **Contact:** a real address.

**Terms** — access is per-account, no sharing; you own your code entirely (`PRD §62` — Afstim claims nothing); we can revoke access for abuse; the service is provided as-is; you're responsible for your own Vercel/Neon/domain accounts and bills.

**Refunds** — you take UPI manually, so: *"Email within 7 days and we'll refund it. No form."* At 20 users that's true and cheap.

**Write these in your own words.** Not legalese. `07A_DESIGN_LANGUAGE` and `PRD §63` both say it: understandable without legal jargon.

## What this module can't do
**I am not a lawyer and this is not legal advice.** Before taking money you likely need a registered entity — sole proprietorship is usually the cheapest route in India. Talk to a CA. It's a few thousand rupees and a few days, and it's the difference between a business and a hobby that takes payments.

## Out of scope
Cookie banner (we have no third-party cookies — one more reason not to add analytics SDKs). GDPR. Data processing agreements.

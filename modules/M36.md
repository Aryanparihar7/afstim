# M36 — Missions 4–6

Track: Authoring · Depends: M35 · **Founder work, ~4 days**
**Mission 4 is where Afstim keeps its promise. Everything else is scaffolding around it.**

## Goal
A learner has a live URL, HTTPS, and a real database in production.

## The three

| # | Slug | Capability | Checks |
|---|---|---|---|
| 04 | `deploy-to-vercel` | **A public URL exists** | `command_succeeds` (build), `remote_http_ok`, `remote_https_valid` |
| 05 | `add-a-database` | Real data, not a JSON file | `command_succeeds` (a query runs) |
| 06 | `env-vars-in-production` | Secrets don't live in code | `remote_http_ok` on `/api/health` → `db: ok` |

## Mission 4 — write this one three times

It is the emotional centre of the product. It's the first time a stranger on the internet can open something they made. Every mission before it is setup; every mission after is depth.

**The moment `remote_http_ok` goes green from their own terminal is what you are selling.** Not the curriculum. That moment.

So: the prose before it should be short and calm. Don't oversell it — let the check do the work.

## The custom domain is not here — and that's deliberate

Vercel's `*.vercel.app` is **already public and already HTTPS.** The promise is kept at mission 4, for ₹0.

Your PRD's Success Criteria requires a custom domain. That means a ~₹1,000/year card purchase, mid-journey, from a BCA student who may not have a card. It would gate your headline outcome behind a payment rail you don't control, for a persona defined by not having one.

**Custom domain is mission 10, and it's optional.** Nobody is blocked from shipping by not owning a domain.

## Mission 5 — the check that will bite you

*"Database connection works"* — how?

- ❌ `file_contains: "PrismaClient"` — checks that they copied your code
- ❌ HTTP-probing `/api/users` — assumes they named a route
- ✅ `command_succeeds: npx prisma db execute --stdin <<< "SELECT 1"` — did a query actually run against a real database?

Outcome, not name. Every time.

## Mission 6 — the one that teaches the most

Env vars is where "it works on my machine" dies. `/api/health` returning `db: ok` from production, when the same code fails locally without `.env`, is the moment the difference between local and production becomes real.

Write from your own log. You hit this. Find where.

## Founder gate — **the real one**

1. `npm run build` passes. ☐
2. Do 1→6 yourself in a fresh folder, following only your words. ☐
3. **Put five real people through missions 1–6.**
4. **Count how many reach a live URL at mission 4.**

**Then ask them for money.**

Not a survey. Not "would you pay?" A number, a UPI QR, and silence. What they do next is the only honest signal you will get in this entire project.

- 3+ of 5 reach mission 4 → the product works
- 3+ of 5 pay → **you have a business.** Build M37.
- Fewer → the missions are wrong, or the price is, or the thing itself is. **Do not build M37 to escape this.** Fix it here. Adding features to avoid a content problem is how the next 18 months disappear.

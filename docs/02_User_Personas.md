# Purpose

This document defines the people ShipLab is designed for.

Every product decision,
every UX decision,
every AI interaction,
every engineering decision,

should reference these personas.

If a feature does not improve the experience for the primary persona,

its inclusion should be questioned.

---

# PRIMARY PERSONA 01

## The Final-Year Student

Age

19–25

Examples

BCA

MCA

B.Tech

Computer Science Undergraduate

Computer Science Postgraduate

---

### Background

Has completed programming courses.

Knows Python.

Possibly knows HTML, CSS and basic JavaScript.

Has created small academic projects.

Has never independently deployed a production-ready application.

---

### Goals

Get placement.

Build a strong portfolio.

Understand deployment.

Become industry-ready.

Gain confidence.

Launch personal ideas.

---

### Frustrations

Too many tutorials.

Too much scattered information.

Doesn't know what to learn next.

Deployment feels intimidating.

Fear of breaking things.

Feels "I know coding but can't build products."

# PRIMARY PERSONA 02

## Self-Taught Developer

Age

18–35

---

### Background

Learned programming independently.

Completed multiple online tutorials.

Often follows YouTube creators.

Has several unfinished GitHub repositories.

Knows coding.

Does not know production.

---

### Goals

Launch side projects.

Freelance.

Build SaaS products.

Become independent.

---

### Frustrations

Tutorial fatigue.

Incomplete knowledge.

Doesn't know DevOps.

Cannot confidently launch products.

---

### Behaviour

Highly curious.

Frequently experiments.

Often starts projects.

Rarely finishes them.

---

### Success Definition

"I can build and deploy software without depending on tutorials."

---

# Common Characteristics

Across all Primary Personas

They know some programming.

They lack deployment confidence.

They want practical experience.

They prefer building over watching.

They value AI guidance.

They need a roadmap.

---

# What Users Are NOT Looking For

Another course.

Another certificate.

Another playlist.

Another coding challenge website.

They want capability.

Not content.

---

# Product Design Implications

The product should:

Reduce cognitive load.

Avoid unnecessary complexity.

Provide immediate feedback.

Celebrate meaningful progress.

Remain professional.

Remain minimal.

Remain focused.

---

# AI Behaviour Implications

The AI Mentor should:

Teach.

Guide.

Question.

Explain.

Review.

Encourage.

The AI Mentor should not:

Complete entire missions automatically.

Hide important concepts.

Remove learning opportunities.

---

# UX Implications

Users should always know:

Where they are.

What they are building.

Why they are building it.

What success looks like.

---

# Engineering Implications

Features should optimize for:

Fast onboarding.

Fast first success.

Reliable progress tracking.

Low friction.

High clarity.

Every engineering decision should improve learner confidence.
